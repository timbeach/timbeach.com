## Article Outline

### Title & Intro
"Releasing Music as a Human Being: From WAV Files to Spotify"

Opening premise: You have mixed/mastered tracks and album art. You have nothing else. Here's the complete checklist.

### The Checklist (TL;DR)

Numbered reference list in recommended execution order:

1. Register with a PRO (BMI or ASCAP)
2. Register with the MLC (free mechanical royalty collection)
3. Prepare audio files (format, sample rate, loudness)
4. Prepare album art (3000x3000, sRGB, square)
5. Decide: single vs. two singles (release strategy)
6. Choose a distributor
7. Upload and fill out metadata
8. Set a release date (4-6 weeks out)
9. Pitch to Spotify editorial playlists
10. Register copyright with US Copyright Office
11. (Optional) Admin publishing for international royalties

### Deep Dive Sections

Each section covers one checklist item with full details, tables, and practical recommendations.

**1. PRO Registration**
- What performance royalties are and why you need a PRO
- ASCAP vs BMI comparison table (cost, payment schedule, contract terms)
- SESAC is invite-only, ignore it
- IPI number: what it is, when you get it
- Recommendation: BMI free for writers, ASCAP cheaper if also registering publisher entity
- Register works immediately; royalties don't collect retroactively

**2. The MLC (Mechanical Licensing Collective)**
- What mechanical royalties are (distinct from performance royalties)
- MLC is free, no commission, 100% of royalties kept
- ~15% of songwriter streaming income goes unclaimed because artists skip this
- Registration process at themlc.com

**3. Audio File Specs**
- Format: FLAC preferred, WAV accepted
- Sample rate: 44.1 kHz minimum, submit at native mastering rate
- Bit depth: 24-bit preferred, 16-bit minimum
- Loudness: Spotify normalizes to -14 LUFS integrated
- True peak ceiling: -1 dBTP (or -2 dBTP if master louder than -14 LUFS)
- Stereo only, no mono or surround

**4. Album Art Specs**
- Minimum: 640x640px, recommended: 3000x3000px
- Square (1:1 aspect ratio) mandatory
- JPG or PNG, sRGB color space, 24-bit color depth
- No upscaling, no URLs/contact info, no pricing references

**5. Single vs. EP vs. Album**
- Spotify classification rules table:
  - Single: 1-3 tracks, all under 10 min, total under 30 min
  - EP: 4-6 tracks under 30 min, OR 1-3 tracks with one 10+ min
  - Album: 7+ tracks OR 30+ min total
- Strategy discussion: two separate singles = two release events, more algorithmic opportunities
- One 2-track single = simpler, makes sense if tracks are thematically linked

**6. Distributor Comparison**
- Comparison table: DistroKid, CD Baby, TuneCore, Amuse, LANDR
- Columns: pricing model, cost, royalty split, time to live, notable features
- Key differences: annual sub (DistroKid $24.99/yr, 100%) vs per-release (CD Baby $9.99, keeps 9%)
- Recommendation based on release frequency

**7. Metadata**
- Required fields table: title, artist, genre, release date, ISRC, UPC, explicit flag, language
- ISRC: one per track, distributor assigns free
- UPC: one per release, distributor assigns free
- P-line and C-line: sound recording and composition copyright lines
- Label name: "Self-Released" or custom

**8. Timeline & Release Strategy**
- Submit 4-6 weeks before target date (industry best practice)
- Minimum 7 days for Spotify editorial playlist pitching
- Pitch through Spotify for Artists (one unreleased track per release)
- Distributor processing times table

**9. Copyright Registration**
- Copyright exists automatically but registration provides legal benefits
- SR form covers both recording and composition when same owner
- eCO online filing: $45 (single author/work) or $65 (standard)
- Registration date = filing date (matters for statutory damages)

**10. Admin Publishing (Optional)**
- What it is vs traditional publishing (no ownership transfer)
- Songtrust: ~$100 setup + 15% commission
- When it's worth it: significant international streams
- MLC handles US mechanicals for free; admin publishing adds international collection

### Closing
Brief wrap-up: "You made the music. The rest is paperwork."
