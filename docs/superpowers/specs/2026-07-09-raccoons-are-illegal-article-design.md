## Facts to get right (from Tim's own memory; overrides Gemini where they differ)
- Heard noises in the wall behind the bed for several nights; caught + relocated an adult
  raccoon; noises continued; late one night found **Charls** on the wrap-around deck's
  handrail and his sister **Doozy** peeking from her spot in the deck/porch ceiling.
  Grabbed a bin + gloves, scooped Charls first, then reached up for Doozy.
- Kits ~6-8 weeks, likely younger. Names spelled **Charls** (not "Charles") and **Doozy**.
  Siblings — Charls male, Doozy female.
- First aid: unflavored **Pedialyte** by dropper to stop the screaming/keep hydrated.
- Food ACTUALLY USED: **Esbilac** (puppy milk replacer), from Tractor Supply, cut with
  Pedialyte at first, transitioning to full formula. Fox Valley 40/25 = the specialized
  gold standard he researched but couldn't get same-day. (Gemini pushed KMR as "ideal,"
  but Esbilac is what Tim used — tell what happened.)
- Care regimen: feed every 3-4 hours; warm-damp-washcloth to stimulate elimination after
  every feeding (kits under 6 wks can't go on their own); microwaved dry-rice tube sock
  for warmth; feed belly-down never on their back (aspiration -> fatal pneumonia).
- Called the local wildlife vet the next morning — they HUNG UP before he could speak.
  So he held the kits and cared for them ~a week.
- ~A week in, called the vet again -> learned the **RVS (Rabies Vector Species)** reality:
  illegal to keep in TN; no live rabies test (only euthanized brain tissue); any reported
  scratch/bite can trigger a state euthanasia order. The little claw-scratches that redden
  but don't break skin = the quiet dread.
- The runaround: TWRA (Region 4) won't call back; a state-listed nature park says, flatly,
  **"let them die."** Contingency he weighed: raise them in secret ~5 months to a fall
  soft-release (predator-proof enclosure, gradual door-open weaning).
- Breakthrough: **Geri Wynn** (licensed RVS rehabber) -> passes the text-only number for
  **Marybeth / Mountain Tails Wildlife Rehab**. Texted photos+info; Marybeth: she'll take
  them. Two hours away, near Knoxville (Corryton).
- The run: ~6:00 PM (18:00) departure, ~8:00 PM arrival. Just **Mason** (Tim's partner &
  fiance, they/them pronouns) and Tim — the boys were with their mother. Carrier lined
  with the kits' towels, fresh warm rice-sock, one last warm bottle. I-81 -> I-40.
- Closing facts: captive raccoons can reach 15-20 yrs vs 2-3 in the wild, BUT that only
  applies to pro zoos/nature centers; raccoons aren't domesticated (that takes millennia),
  hit a "Jekyll & Hyde" hormonal shift ~1 yr (biting, destruction), and pet-kept adults
  usually end up caged for safety — 15-20 yrs caged is a worse life than a shorter wild
  one. Handing them to a rehab = the wild life nature designed them for.

## Thesis (the line the whole piece serves)
Laws written to protect public health end up punishing compassion — leaving ordinary
people to choose between breaking the law, driving a hundred miles, or watching an animal
die.
